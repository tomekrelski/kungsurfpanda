My journey with meditation began at the age of 12, when I started practicing Aikido and Yoga. That early exposure sparked a deep interest in mindfulness and inner awareness.

I was fortunate to learn from experienced masters in the Himalayan tradition, where I studied Pranayama, Yoga Nidra, and various meditation techniques.

A true turning point came in 2018, when I completed my first 10-day Vipassana retreat in Thailand — a life-changing experience that reshaped my daily habits. I followed it up with a second retreat one year later.

Since that first Vipassana, meditation has become an essential part of my life. I’ve maintained a consistent daily practice, meditating for at least 10 minutes every single day.

I'm also deeply interested in breathwork, which helps me reach deeper meditative states, as well as dynamic body-mind practices like ecstatic dance.
