My inner journey started at 12, when I was doing Aikido, yoga, and probably asking deeper questions than most adults.

I was lucky to learn from real-deal Himalayan masters who showed me the ropes of Pranayama, Yoga Nidra, and other ways to sit still without falling asleep.

But the real game-changer came in 2018: a 10-day silent Vipassana retreat in Thailand. No phone, no talking, no eye contact just me, my thoughts, up to 20 hours of meditation a day, and a bunch of mosquito bites. It totally rewired my brain in the best way. I even did it again the next year (yes, on purpose).

Since then, meditation isn’t just something I do; it’s part of who I am. Every day, at least 10 minutes of sitting, observing my breath. It’s like drilling a hole in rock slow, steady, but over time, the change is inevitable.

I’m also hooked on breathwork, ecstatic dance, and any practice that helps me feel more balanced, happier, still, and open-minded. It’s all about feeling more human, grounded, and in tune with the present moment.
