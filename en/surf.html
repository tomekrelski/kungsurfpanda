I rode my first wave in 2008 at Balneario, Tarifa and that was it. One perfect glide, and I was completely hooked. I still remember the feeling: part magic, part panic, and 100% life-changing. From that day on, I wasn’t just riding waves I was riding straight into a lifestyle of salty hair, sandy gear, and chasing stoke ever since.

In 2009, I joined the Erasmus program in Las Palmas de Gran Canaria where I somehow managed to surf every single day for 10 months… and get paid for it. Legendary student exchange!

Thanks to seasonal jobs (and some creative budgeting), I chased waves around the world from Morocco and Portugal to Peru, Bali, and beyond. Every windless day in Tarifa became a surf mission.

I love riding all kinds of waves, on all kinds of boards short, long, soft, weird-shaped... if it floats, I’ll surf it.

When the sea’s sleeping, I train on land with surfskate, slackline, balance boards, and mobility drills because you can always be a bit smoother, faster, and funnier on a wave. For me, surfing isn't just a sport it's a way of life (and an excuse to always be sandy and salty).
